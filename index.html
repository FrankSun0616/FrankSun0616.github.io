Research profile

I am a physics researcher whose public work moves between experimental particle physics, machine-learning-driven analysis, cosmology, and astrophysics. The most current institutional record places me at HKUST as a PhD student in Physics, while earlier training and publications connect to Brown University and Macalester College.

The publication record shows a genuinely interdisciplinary arc: a recent redshift-survey paper, work in stellar evolution and nucleosynthesis, a theory paper in Gauss-Bonnet cosmology with torsion, and earlier solo work on metallicity-dependent isotopic abundances. That mix is more interesting than a generic "ML innovator" label, so the site now foregrounds it directly.
